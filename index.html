History


Introduction: The Spark of Ilandhoothu


In 1987, during the second-year college curriculum, a course titled "Introduction to Journalism" became the turning point in the life of Radha Krishnan. It was through this course that he was first introduced to the world of journalism, sparking a passion that would change the course of his academic life. The course was taught by Professor K. Sengaiyan, the head of the Tamil Department, whose teaching style deeply inspired Radha Krishnan. Under the guidance of Professor Sengaiyan, Radha Krishnan was encouraged to further pursue journalism.

The Founder of Illanthoodhu
"Mr.Radha Krishnan"


A New Beginning: Launching the Journal


Radha Krishnan, feeling the need to create a space for student expression, shared his idea with Professor Venkatasalam, the head of the Business Studies Department. He received further encouragement from him. The initial steps towards launching a student-run journal began, and the project was named "Amritharajagam," under a pen name. Radha Krishnan was motivated to continue learning about journalism and eventually decided that the journal should be a long-term project.

The Growth of the Journal: A Student - Centric Publication


With the help of Professor Sengaiyan, Radha Krishnan approached the principal of the college, Dr. R. Balasubramanian, seeking approval to start the journal. The principal agreed, provided that the journal did not reflect negatively on the professors or the college. Radha Krishnan was asked to document his plans, and he eagerly set to work, ensuring that the journal would showcase the writing talents of the students.

Funding and Distribution: Managing the First Print Run


The first edition of Ilandhoothu was released during the college's annual festival, marking the official debut of the journal. The layout, color scheme, and publication details were carefully considered, including the use of yellow in the design. The magazine was printed and sold, with proceeds used to fund the college’s annual events.

Leadership Transition: Empowering the Next Generation


As Radha Krishnan looked to the future, he recognized the importance of a smooth leadership transition. He mentored Aruna Ramesh, who took over as the editor, preparing her for the challenges of running the journal. Even though Aruna had no prior knowledge of journalism, Radha Krishnan's guidance helped her develop the necessary skills.

Conclusion: A Legacy of Student Expression


The journey of Ilandhoothu is a testament to the power of student initiative, leadership, and expression. What began as a small project in 1987 has grown into a platform that continues to give students a voice in their academic community, encouraging creativity and fostering a sense of collaboration.
